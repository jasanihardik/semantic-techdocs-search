Corporate Remote Work & Hybrid Work Policy
1. Overview

This policy defines expectations, eligibility, and security requirements for employees operating in a remote or hybrid work environment. The purpose is to support flexibility while ensuring productivity, data protection, and consistent communication standards.

2. Eligibility

Remote work approval depends on:

Role suitability

Manager approval

Performance record

Compliance with technical and security requirements

Some roles (e.g., lab technicians, facility operations) may require physical presence.

3. Work Hours & Availability

Employees must maintain the following:

Core online hours: 10 AM – 3 PM local time

Total working hours should match the employee’s normal schedule

Status on internal communication tools must be accurate

Meetings must be attended virtually unless otherwise requested

Employees working across time zones should align with team schedules when possible.

4. Equipment & Workspace Standards
4.1 Corporate Equipment

Employees must use:

Company-provided laptop or desktop

Encrypted storage devices

Corporate VPN

Approved video conferencing tools

Personal devices should only be used if explicitly approved by IT.

4.2 Workspace Requirements

A remote workspace must:

Be quiet and suitable for meetings

Allow secure handling of company data

Prevent access from unauthorized individuals (family, roommates, etc.)

Use of public spaces (cafés, airports) for work is discouraged except for short periods.

5. Connectivity & Technical Requirements

Remote employees are responsible for:

Reliable high-speed internet

Using VPN for all internal system access

Ensuring antivirus and OS updates are current

Reporting hardware issues immediately

If internet outages occur, employees must notify their manager as early as possible.

6. Data Security & Compliance
6.1 Mandatory Practices

Employees must adhere to:

VPN usage at all times

Screen locking when away

No printing of sensitive documents at home unless approved

No storing corporate files on personal cloud services

6.2 Prohibited Behaviors

Employees must NOT:

Share access with family members

Leave company equipment unattended in public

Disable security tools (MFA, VPN, antivirus)

Download unauthorized software

Violations may result in disciplinary action.

7. Travel & Cross-Border Work

If working remotely outside your home province or country, employees must:

Inform HR and IT

Confirm compliance with data residency regulations

Avoid using unsecured foreign networks

Follow export control policies

Working internationally without approval is not permitted.

8. Communication Expectations

Daily check-ins may be required depending on the team

Cameras should be enabled during key meetings

Teams should use shared digital collaboration tools

Response time to emails/messages should follow internal standards

9. Health, Safety & Ergonomics

Employees should maintain:

A safe, ergonomic workspace

Regular breaks

Compliance with health and safety guidelines

Reporting of any remote-work-related injuries

The company may provide ergonomic assessments upon request.

10. Summary

This policy promotes trust, flexibility, and productivity while maintaining security, compliance, and collaboration across distributed teams.
